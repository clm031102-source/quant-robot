## Purpose

Round421 audits whether the Round419 aggressive entry-timed candidate is truly ready for paper simulation.

The finding is important: aggregate event-level entry timing is not enough. A paper simulator needs signal rows that can be generated by entry cohort, not only by exit-date aggregate returns.

## Problem Found

Signal reconstruction of the Round419 aggregate event stream reconciled returns almost exactly:

- max absolute reconciliation difference: `3.5388358909926865e-16`
- sum absolute reconciliation difference: `9.714198973292136e-14`

But it was not paper-ready:

- `event_decision_date_mismatch`
- `event_decision_date_collapses_multiple_trade_decisions`
- `trade_pairs_missing_event_exposure`

The source event stream has 834 unique exit dates, while the trade-level reconstruction shows many exit dates contain multiple entry decisions. The old aggregate stream therefore cannot be used as a direct paper-simulation signal.
